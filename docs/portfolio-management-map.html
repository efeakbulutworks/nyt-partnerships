Section 02 · The New York Times Advertising Partnerships

Portfolio Management Map

A shared evaluation language Dao and I can use to decide what deserves organizational energy. The map evaluates partnerships across three dimensions — Strategic Value, Commercial Value, and Resource Intensity — using a consistent set of metrics. It enables internal alignment, prioritization, resource planning, and pipeline forecasting.

Step 1 — Strategic Evaluation

Metric Evaluation Prompt Input Type
Growth Potential Can this drive subscriber growth, retention, or audience engagement? 1–5 score slider
Audience Expansion Does this reach new audiences, categories, or communities? 1–5 score slider
Brand Alignment Does this strengthen NYT's premium positioning and values? 1–5 score slider
Cross-Brand Ecosystem Which NYT properties participate? Multi-select
Innovation Value Does this introduce new partnership models, formats, or capabilities? 1–5 score slider

Step 2 — Commercial Evaluation

Metric Evaluation Prompt Input Type
Revenue Potential What is the estimated annual revenue potential? Numeric ($)
Production Cost What is the estimated production and execution cost? Numeric ($)
Margin Efficiency Auto-calculated from revenue vs cost Auto-calculated
Scalability Can this framework be reused, expanded, or repeated efficiently? 1–5 score slider
Longevity Potential One-time activation or long-term repeatable relationship? 1–5 score slider

Step 3 — Resource Intensity Evaluation

Metric Evaluation Prompt Input Type
Production Complexity How operationally complex is execution? 1–5 score slider
Cross-Functional Coordination How many teams and stakeholders are required? 1–5 score slider
Timeline / Lead Time How long is the planning and execution cycle? 1–5 score slider
Staffing Requirements How much ongoing team bandwidth is required? 1–5 score slider
Custom Asset Development Does this require bespoke creative, tech, or editorial development? 1–5 score slider
