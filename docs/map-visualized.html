Section 03 · The New York Times Advertising Partnerships

The Map, Visualized

Each bubble represents a partnership archetype plotted across three dimensions simultaneously: Strategic Value (x-axis), Commercial Value (y-axis), and Resource Intensity (bubble size — larger means more resource-intensive). Color indicates which area of the NYT ecosystem the partnership primarily serves.

The upper-right quadrant is the portfolio sweet spot: high strategic and commercial value. The goal is to migrate more partnerships there over time while managing resource intensity.

Reading the Chart

Partnerships in the upper-right are priorities — high strategic and commercial value. Those in the lower-right may be strong brand plays that need commercial packaging. Those in the upper-left are revenue contributors that may not be deeply strategic. The lower-left warrants the hardest scrutiny.

Resource intensity (bubble size) tells you the cost of opportunity. A large bubble in the upper-right is worth the investment. A large bubble in the lower-left is a signal to restructure or sunset.
